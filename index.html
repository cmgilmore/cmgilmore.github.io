How I Work

How I Work

Therapy with me is collaborative and tailored — we figure out together what works for you. I draw on a range of evidence-based approaches depending on what you're navigating:

EMDR

An evidence-based approach that helps process and heal distressing experiences, reducing the emotional charge connected to difficult memories.

Trauma-Informed Care

Understanding how past experiences shape present patterns, with deep respect for how the nervous system learned to survive.

Somatic Techniques

Bringing awareness to the body's signals and sensations as a pathway to processing and healing stored stress and trauma.

Mindfulness-Based Approaches

Building present-moment awareness to create space between experience and reaction — and to reconnect with what matters.

Nervous System Awareness

Working with your nervous system — not against it — to move from survival states toward safety, connection, and regulation.

Sessions are a space to explore what's hard, identify patterns, and build toward what you actually want your life to look like.

Who I Help

I work with adults and couples navigating anxiety, burnout, trauma, relationship challenges, emotional overwhelm, identity questions, life transitions, and the long-term effects of chronic stress.

Anxiety & Chronic Stress

Feeling mentally "on" all the time. Overthinking, tension, irritability, sleep issues, or a nervous system that rarely feels fully settled.

Burnout & Exhaustion

Feeling depleted, disconnected, or emotionally numb after years of managing responsibilities while neglecting your own needs.

Trauma & Nervous System

Struggling with conflict, communication, emotional closeness, trust, or recurring dynamics that leave you feeling stuck. I also work with couples.

Men's Mental Health

Helping men move beyond isolation, emotional shutdown, and survival-mode living toward self-awareness, connection, and authenticity.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What can I expect from our first session?+
Our first session is a chance to get to know each other. We'll talk about what brought you to therapy, what you're hoping to work on, and I'll answer any questions you have about my approach. There's no pressure — you set the pace.
How long does therapy typically last?+
That depends on what you're working toward. Some people find meaningful change in 8–12 sessions; others benefit from longer-term work. We'll check in regularly and adjust based on how things are going.
Do you accept insurance, and what does a session cost?+
I am a private-pay practice and do not bill insurance directly. I can provide a superbill if you'd like to seek reimbursement from your insurance provider. Sessions are $150 for 50 minutes.
Do you offer telehealth?+
Yes — I offer telehealth sessions for clients in Colorado. All you need is a private space and a reliable internet connection.
What if therapy doesn't feel like the right fit?+
It's important to work with someone you feel comfortable with. If after a few sessions it doesn't feel like the right match, I'll support you in finding a therapist who is. Your care comes first.
